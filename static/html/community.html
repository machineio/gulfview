THE Community

Costa Rica is a small Central American country located at 10° latitude north of the equator with a population of 4.5 million inhabitants, with an area of approx.20,000 miles.², roughly the size of West Virginia. However, due to its geographical position, this small Central American country holds about 5% of the biodiversity of the entire planet and therefore considered one the most diverse places on earth by biomass. Costa Rica is divided into seven provinces, of which Guanacaste is considered by the Ticos (The way Costa Ricans are known) one of the most beautiful areas of the of country. Costa Rica borders North with Nicaragua and Southeast with Panama. To the West is the beautiful and warm Pacific Ocean and to the East the Caribbean Sea. This small Central American nation has become a pioneer leader in conservation on the continent due to its remarkable efforts in preservation of its wild-life, Costa Rica has declared approx. 30% of its national territory to national parks, giving this country a great deal of international recognition. Costa Rica is not only known as one of the richest countries in the world in regards to flora and fauna, but is also known as one of the oldest democracies in Latin America and has one of the most stable political environments of the region, boasting a very high educational level. Since the country abolished the army back on December 1, 1948 the country has dedicated a great deal of its national budget to education with a literacy rate of 95% of one the highest of Central America.

Guanacaste Province

Guanacaste has not only the most scenic and beautiful beach areas of Costa Rica; it also maintains the most significant ecological site of tropical dry forest. The tropical forest of Guanacaste is considered of international importance since it’s a Unesco World Heritage site as of 1999.

Jicaral

This little Northwest town of Costa Rica is just located 12 kilometers form the ferry that comes across the beautiful pacific body of water known as the gulf of Nicoya. Jicaral is known by its amazing Pacific lowland topography with a small town near by…..with the hardiest and friendliest working community ….a remarkable agricultural land characterized by the sugar cane farming…. corn, beans as well as cattle pasturelands, it has the lowest rainfall of the country, best tropical weather all year round….without forgetting its outstanding tropical fishing.
